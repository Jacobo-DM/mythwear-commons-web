About MythWear

MythWear is a commons-rooted project exploring wearable movement medicine: garments, hoods, masks, braids, ropes, bells, tactile anchors, and low-tech guidance patterns meant to help humans return to presence, rhythm, and embodied calm. These designs arise from walking, teaching, rage dancing, listening, and noticing how the body remembers when the world forgets.

This release includes:

  • MythWear Hood (three-point frame, braided brow, temple anchors, arc–spine line, attachable bells, beads, scents, or glowlights)
  • RootMask / RootFace design family (nopal-inspired warmth, protective shading, soft-light lattice)
  • Leaf Circuit / Spine Line — a wearable nervous-system metaphor running from brow to spine
  • Sensorless cues (weighted seams, directional braids, textured haptics, quiet bells, sound-thread guidance)
  • Low-tech, repairable, Commons-first principles
  • All current text schematics and pattern logic
